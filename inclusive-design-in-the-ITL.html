Our Inclusive Design Practice in the Inclusive Tech Lab

Inclusive design in the Inclusive Tech Lab starts with a simple premise: disability is not a segment to analyze, it is a reality to engage with directly. We co-design with people with disabilities, not to validate ideas, but to shape them, because left to ourselves, our assumptions will always narrow who we build for. Inclusive design sprints bring design, engineering, and product teams into that proximity, grounding decisions in lived experience rather than abstraction. This is not a workshop or a checkpoint. It is how we build with people, not just for them.

  • Ideally this activity is done for 2-3 days, but activities can be done asynchronously if needed.
  • The Inclusive Tech Lab can accommodate 20-24 sprint participants in person, with as many as you would like over Teams.
    • Participants should be invited a month before the sprint for the time commitment.
    • Participants can be designers, PMs, marketers, engineers, support or anyone involved in creating things that touch our customers.
  • We invite 3-6 disabled advisors to work with our sprint participants.
    • Conversing with advisors is an activity that cannot be missed by participants.
    • They can be remote, but in person is better.
    • They can be colleagues, but external experts are preferred.
    • We provide our advisors with a gratuity for their time.
  • Producing a wrap-up video is an excellent opportunity to share ideas with the broader team.

Introduction to Microsoft Inclusive Design

Microsoft's inclusive design approach begins by recognizing exclusion, often shaped by our own biases, and treating it as a signal for better ideas. Grounded in "nothing about us without us," we design with disability communities to create solutions that extend to many.

Inclusive Design material

Recognize Exclusion

Recognize Exclusion highlights where our designs unintentionally create barriers. By examining moments that demand extra effort, physical or cognitive, we can uncover and address the gaps that limit who our products truly serve.

Learn from Diversity

Learn from Diversity recognizes that real insight comes from lived experience, not assumption. By working alongside disability communities, we uncover barriers, challenge bias, and shape solutions that reflect real needs.

Solve for One, Extend to Many

Solve for One, Extend to Many recognizes that meaningful innovation starts with individuals, not averages. By solving for a specific need first, we create solutions that can scale and improve experiences for many.

Solve Agent

This two-hour UXCon Vienna workshop shares the foundations of Inclusive Design and how to run effective sprints. Each time we teach, we sharpen the practice, making it more usable, repeatable, and accessible to designers everywhere.

Devices + Accessories + Augmentations

People engage with devices in countless ways, and design must meet that diversity. By focusing on clear, operable, and understandable interactions, and co-designing with disabled communities, we have created a system of devices, accessories, and augmentations that can be tailored to fit individual needs.
