Examples of potential exclusions for Video Games - Inclusive Tech Lab

Ability activation with cooldown

User triggers an ability on a face/number button; must wait for cooldown before reuse. — Genre: Action RPG

Perceivable

  • Tiny cooldown timers; color-only ready state.
  • Cooldown shading is subtle and monochrome; ready state isn’t perceivable.

Operable

  • Requires chorded inputs; no remap or accessibility shortcut.
  • Double-tap or press-and-hold requirements cause frequent misfires; no single-tap option.

Understandable

  • Ability effects and synergies aren’t described.
  • Global vs. per-ability cooldown stacking isn’t surfaced; timing is confusing.

Aim down sights

User holds the aim trigger/mouse right-click to enter precision aim mode. — Genre: FPS/TPS

Perceivable

  • Reticle and scope markings have poor contrast; targets are hard to discern.
  • Scope bloom and glint obscure the center dot on bright backgrounds.

Operable

  • High sensitivity and no aim-assist options; micro-aiming is inaccessible.
  • ADS sensitivity can’t be set independently from hip-fire; fine control is impossible for some players.

Understandable

  • ADS vs. hip-fire accuracy rules aren’t taught; players misjudge effect.
  • Sway, breath-hold, and bullet drop rules aren’t surfaced; players misjudge shots.

Alternate fire / Heavy attack

User holds or presses the secondary input for a charged/heavy/special strike. — Genre: Shooter/Action

Perceivable

  • Charge glow/sound is subtle; can’t perceive ready state.
  • Charge meter sits in a peripheral HUD corner; hard to perceive while aiming.

Operable

  • Requires precise hold timing or chorded inputs; no remap.
  • Requires simultaneous aim + charge + movement; high-dexterity chord with no remap.

Understandable

  • Alternate effects (pierce, AoE) aren’t described; outcomes unclear.
  • Elemental interactions (e.g., heavy staggers shields) aren’t explained anywhere.

Analog stick locomotion

User tilts the left stick to walk/run; tilt magnitude controls speed. — Genre: Action-Adventure

Perceivable

  • Low-contrast terrain cues and heavy motion blur hide hazards and paths.
  • Strong head-bob, film grain, or vignette obscure path markers and hazards in dim scenes.

Operable

  • Small dead-zone/acceleration curve requires fine motor control; no remap or assist.
  • Turn acceleration tied to frame rate makes rotation inconsistent on low-FPS systems.

Understandable

  • No compass or route guidance; players can’t tell where locomotion should head.
  • Encumbrance or terrain penalties silently alter speed; movement feels inconsistent without explanation.

Block / Guard / Parry

User holds block to reduce damage or taps with timing to parry incoming hits. — Genre: Action RPG

Perceivable

  • Enemy telegraphs are faint/fast; windows hard to see.
  • Parry cue is audio-only; no visual alternative under heavy effects.

Operable

  • Frame-perfect parries required; no input buffer/timing aid.
  • Requires release-and-press within tight frames; no input buffer or timing assist.

Understandable

  • Stamina/guard-break consequences are not explained.
  • Chip damage, guard stamina, and parry failure penalties are not explained.

Building / Placement grid

User enters build mode; cycles piece types and places them on a snap grid. — Genre: Sandbox/Survival

Perceivable

  • Grid lines/snapping guides have low contrast.
  • Snap/rotate indicators are tiny; players can’t see alignment.

Operable

  • Rotate/flip uses complex chords; no single-button alternatives.
  • Rotate bound to mouse wheel or complex chords; no on-screen buttons.

Understandable

  • Snapping/overlap rules aren’t documented; placements surprise players.
  • Stability/structural rules (supports, load) are hidden; collapses feel random.

Context-sensitive interact

User presses “Interact” near an object/NPC to open doors, pick up items, or activate switches. — Genre: Action-Adventure

Perceivable

  • Small, low-contrast prompt icon; interaction point blends into scenery.
  • Interaction prompts are occluded by subtitles/crosshair clutter.

Operable

  • Narrow activation cone; requires pixel-precise positioning.
  • Some objects require tap vs. others require hold; inconsistent dwell times block reliable action.

Understandable

  • Vague labels like “Use” or “Interact” don’t convey outcomes.
  • Multiple verbs share one key with no preview; outcome (loot vs. talk vs. disarm) is unclear.

Crafting / Upgrade at station

User interacts with a bench; navigates a recipe tree and confirms craft/upgrade. — Genre: Survival/Crafting

Perceivable

  • Recipe icons and rarities are visually similar.
  • Semi-transparent panels over busy backgrounds reduce recipe text legibility.

Operable

  • Menu navigation requires precise cursor or drag; no list mode.
  • Drag-only ingredient placement; no click-to-add or keyboard selection.

Understandable

  • Resource requirements and outcomes aren’t explained plainly.
  • Success rates, failure risk, and durability changes are not explained before commit.

Drag-to-aim special puzzle piece

User drags from a striped/wrapped piece to choose the clear direction or target line. — Genre: Match-3 Puzzle

Perceivable

  • Trajectory preview is faint on bright boards.
  • Dotted trajectory is too faint against patterned boards; aim is hard to see.

Operable

  • Long drag path needed; hard for limited mobility; no button choice.
  • Target snaps to unintended lane; no fine adjustment buttons for accuracy.

Understandable

  • Special piece effects and targeting rules unclear.
  • Orientation and blast radius for specials aren’t explained before use.

Driving controls

User accelerates/brakes and steers with triggers/stick or keys; handbrake for tight turns. — Genre: Racing

Perceivable

  • Small HUD for speed/gear; track cues unclear.
  • Night tracks lack readable edge markers; brake lights and signage are dim.

Operable

  • Analog triggers mandatory; no assist for digital inputs.
  • Forced steering assist or traction control can’t be disabled; precision sufferers can’t operate as intended.

Understandable

  • Off-road/traction rules are unstated; crashes feel random.
  • Corner-cutting penalties and ghost car rules aren’t surfaced; disqualifications surprise players.

Emote / Communication wheel

User opens an emote wheel and selects an emote or quick chat line. — Genre: Multiplayer

Perceivable

  • Emote icons are ambiguous at small sizes.
  • Team/channel indicators are color-only; hard to perceive for color-blind users.

Operable

  • Radial selection times out quickly; hard for limited mobility.
  • Large stick deadzone causes overshoot; small slices are hard to select reliably.

Understandable

  • Team visibility and channel scope (team/global) are unclear.
  • Visibility scope (party, proximity, global) is undocumented; messages go to the wrong audience.

Finisher / Execution

User activates a context prompt on staggered enemies to perform a high-impact move. — Genre: Action

Perceivable

  • On-screen prompt is obscured by VFX.
  • Prompt spawns under blood/debris VFX; unreadable at the moment of availability.

Operable

  • Requires precise camera/position; brief window; no assist.
  • Requires a simultaneous press + directional input; small movement cancels the finisher.

Understandable

  • Alert/aggro consequences of finishers are not described.
  • Player vulnerability during animation isn’t disclosed; risky use is confusing.

Flight controls

User pitches/rolls/yaws with stick/mouse or keys; throttle/boost to change speed. — Genre: Flight/Space Sim

Perceivable

  • Horizon/attitude indicators are faint; depth cues weak.
  • Pitch ladder and horizon lines blend with the skybox; altitude cues are weak.

Operable

  • Invert/tuning missing; fine control inaccessible.
  • Continuous pitch hold required; no trim/autopilot assist for sustained climbs.

Understandable

  • Stall/boost/altitude systems not explained.
  • Stall, flap, and boost interactions aren’t explained; players misinterpret warnings.

Ghost preview before release

User holds a swap to preview the resulting match; releasing commits or sliding back cancels. — Genre: Match-3 Puzzle

Perceivable

  • Preview overlay too translucent; outcome hard to see.
  • Preview path color matches blocker colors; outcome is hard to perceive.

Operable

  • Hold required; no toggle to lock preview; accidental slips commit.
  • Continuous press for multi-second previews causes fatigue; no lock-preview toggle.

Understandable

  • Preview/cancel behavior isn’t taught; users don’t know how to revert.
  • Preview shows only immediate match; cascade probability isn’t explained, leading to surprises.

Hotbar item use

User presses a mapped slot key/button to instantly consume/use the item in that slot. — Genre: RPG

Perceivable

  • Active slot highlight is low contrast; state unclear.
  • Slot numbers and icons are unreadable at couch distance or 4K without UI scale.

Operable

  • Small controller mappings; accidental misfires; no confirm for costly items.
  • Using items while moving demands claw grips; no accessible alternate mapping.

Understandable

  • Cooldowns/limited charges are not surfaced.
  • Icon colors (e.g., blue/purple bottles) don’t map to effects; meaning isn’t taught.

Inventory quick-select wheel

User holds a modifier to open a radial menu and tilts/points to select an item. — Genre: Action RPG

Perceivable

  • Small labels/icons on the wheel are hard to read.
  • Low-contrast wheel over bright scenes; segment labels become illegible.

Operable

  • Hold-and-tilt demands dexterity; no D-pad/keyboard alternative.
  • Radial requires steady hold and precise angle; game doesn’t pause, increasing pressure.

Understandable

  • Time dilation/pausing not indicated; players misjudge selection pressure.
  • Mutually exclusive items (e.g., shield vs. torch) aren’t indicated; choices surprise players.

Lock-on targeting

User toggles lock-on to focus camera and attacks on the nearest target; flick to switch targets. — Genre: Action RPG

Perceivable

  • Lock-on reticle is subtle; target status unclear in clutter.
  • Highlight color clashes with enemy palettes; color-blind players can’t see the target.

Operable

  • Fixed target-cycle with no rebind or sensitivity; hard to operate.
  • Lock breaks on minor occlusion with no sticky setting; target switching is buried.

Understandable

  • Lock-break conditions and target priority rules aren’t taught.
  • Rules for when camera vs. target lock takes priority aren’t taught.

Manual camera control

User moves the right stick or mouse to orbit or look around independently of movement. — Genre: 3D Action-Adventure

Perceivable

  • Motion blur and low-contrast landmarks hinder orientation.
  • Narrow FOV and cluttered HUD obscure sightlines and landmarks.

Operable

  • No inversion/separate X/Y sensitivity; camera fights player input.
  • Nonlinear acceleration curve with no separate X/Y tuning; camera overshoots targets.

Understandable

  • Camera reset and centering rules aren’t introduced.
  • Re-center/soft-lock rules are hidden; players don’t know how to recover the view.

Map open & waypoint set

User opens the map; moves a cursor to set/remove custom waypoints. — Genre: Open-World Adventure

Perceivable

  • Map colors/legends are hard to read; POIs indistinct.
  • Waypoint color blends with biome tones; markers disappear in certain regions.

Operable

  • Cursor speed too fast; edge-scroll only; no search/keyboard entry.
  • Cursor acceleration is high with no keyboard panning or search; fine placement is hard.

Understandable

  • Fast-travel/unlock conditions aren’t communicated.
  • Fast-travel costs and unlock prerequisites (e.g., campfire activation) aren’t stated.

Mount / Vehicle enter & exit

User presses interact near a mount/vehicle to board; presses again to dismount. — Genre: Open-World RPG

Perceivable

  • Prompt blends with environment; mount state unclear.

Operable

  • Requires exact position and facing; no generous activation zone.

Understandable

  • Dismount rules and combat restrictions aren’t taught.
  • Town/instance restrictions on mounting aren’t communicated ahead of time.
